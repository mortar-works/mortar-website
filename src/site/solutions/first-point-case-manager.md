## First Point Case Manager

Open cases, assess needs, and manage support from the very first contact — not after the fact.

**Our First Point Case Manager** is a case management platform designed for the front line of service delivery. Rather than treating case management as something that begins after a complex intake process, the tool is designed to open a case and begin gathering structured data from the moment of first contact — whether that happens in person, by phone, or online. This early, structured engagement improves the quality of decision-making, enables earlier intervention, and creates the data foundation for effective ongoing case management.

### How it Works

First Point Case Manager integrates triage and assessment directly into the case creation process. As a practitioner or user works through the initial assessment, a case is opened automatically — capturing structured need data, generating recommended actions, and enabling the case to be prioritised and monitored from the outset.

- **Instant case creation:** Cases are opened at the point of first contact, not after a separate administrative process.
- **Integrated triage:** Needs assessment is built into the case creation workflow, generating structured data and personalised recommendations from the start.
- **Risk stratification:** Cases are automatically stratified by risk level based on assessment responses, enabling practitioners to prioritise their caseload effectively.
- **Referral and resource management:** Refer cases to internal teams, external services, or national resource directories directly from the case record.

## Key Features

### First Contact Case Opening
The platform is built around the principle that the most important moment in a service journey is the first one. By opening a case at the point of first contact — and embedding assessment within that process — the tool ensures that no information is lost, no need goes unrecorded, and no opportunity for early intervention is missed.

### Integrated Triage and Assessment
Custom triage forms are built into the case creation workflow. As practitioners work through the assessment with a user, the platform applies algorithms to generate recommended resources, flag risk factors, and suggest priority actions — reducing the cognitive load on practitioners and improving the consistency of decisions across a team.

### Risk Monitoring and Prioritisation
A real-time dashboard gives supervisors and managers a clear view of caseload risk across the team. Cases are stratified by urgency and risk level, enabling resources to be directed to where they are most needed. Automated alerts flag cases that have not been reviewed recently or where risk indicators have escalated.

### Referral and Resource Integration
Refer users to internal teams, specialist services, or national and local resource directories directly from the case record. Referrals are tracked, outcomes recorded, and the full referral history maintained as part of the case timeline — creating a comprehensive record of every touchpoint and action.

### Data Portability and Integration
We work with partners to integrate the platform with existing case management, housing, and social care systems — improving data portability and reducing the duplication of data entry. Structured data from the platform can be shared across departments and partner organisations, supporting joined-up decision-making.

### Reporting and Oversight
Access detailed reporting on case volumes, risk profiles, referral outcomes, and intervention effectiveness. Give senior leaders and commissioners the evidence they need to understand service demand, demonstrate impact, and allocate resources more effectively.

## Benefits

- **Earlier, better-informed intervention**
   Structured data captured at first contact enables earlier identification of need and more targeted support — reducing the likelihood of crisis and the cost of late intervention.

- **Consistent, equitable decision-making**
   Integrated triage and algorithm-driven recommendations reduce variability in how practitioners respond to similar needs — improving consistency and fairness across a team.

- **Reduced administrative burden**
   Opening cases and capturing structured data as part of the assessment process — rather than as a separate step — reduces duplication and administrative overhead, freeing practitioners to focus on the work that matters.

- **Comprehensive audit trail**
   Every assessment, referral, and action is recorded and timestamped, creating a full audit trail that supports accountability, learning, and regulatory compliance.
