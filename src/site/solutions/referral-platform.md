## Referral Platform

Connect people to the right support through structured, coordinated digital referral pathways.

**Our Referral Platform** enables organisations to build and manage digital referral pathways that connect people with the services, programmes, and opportunities that best meet their needs. Whether referrals are made by practitioners, self-initiated by users, or triggered across a multi-agency network, the platform creates a consistent, trackable flow of people from first contact to appropriate support — generating the outcome data that funders, commissioners, and partners need.

### How it Works

The platform supports multiple referral routes — self-referral, practitioner referral, and onward referral between partner organisations — within a single coordinated environment. Referrals are structured, tracked, and linked to outcome measures, creating a transparent record of how people move through and between services.

- **Multi-pathway referral:** Support self-referral, assisted referral, and cross-agency onward referral from a single platform.
- **Structured intake:** Capture the information needed to match individuals to appropriate services and ensure smooth handoffs.
- **Real-time tracking:** Monitor referral status, follow up with individuals, and identify those who need additional support to engage.
- **Outcome measurement:** Link referrals to outcome data, building an evidence base for the impact of services and programmes.

## Key Features

### Flexible Referral Pathways
Design referral journeys that reflect the complexity of your service landscape. The platform supports direct referrals to specific services, multi-option matching based on individual need, and structured onward referral between partner organisations — all within a coherent, managed workflow.

### Co-Designed User Experience
Referral processes work best when they are designed with the people who will use them. We build referral platforms through co-design, ensuring that language, navigation, and accessibility reflect the needs of the specific communities being served — including those with neurodivergent needs, complex circumstances, or lower digital confidence.

### Cross-Sector Coordination
Break down the barriers between health, social care, cultural, and community services. The platform provides a shared environment in which organisations from different sectors can manage and respond to referrals, reducing duplication and improving the quality of handoffs.

### Integrated Wellbeing and Outcome Measurement
Embed outcome measurement directly into the referral journey. Collect wellbeing data at the point of referral and at subsequent milestones, building a longitudinal picture of change over time. This generates the consistent, comparable evidence that funders and commissioners increasingly require.

### Impact Reporting
Access real-time data on referral volumes, completion rates, and outcomes across your network. Understand which pathways are working, where people are dropping out, and what the referral pattern tells you about unmet need in your population.

## Benefits

- **Reduced fragmentation**
   Replace informal, inconsistent referral practices with a structured digital process that works the same way across every partner organisation — reducing the gaps and duplications that let people fall through.

- **Better access for those most in need**
   A well-designed referral platform removes barriers to access — making it easier for practitioners to refer and for individuals to self-refer, regardless of their digital confidence or circumstances.

- **Evidence of impact**
   Every referral generates structured data. Linked to outcome measures, this builds a robust evidence base that demonstrates the value of your programmes to funders, commissioners, and policy audiences.

- **Scalable infrastructure**
   A modular platform architecture means the platform can be extended to new partners, geographies, and service types — making it a foundation for system-level change rather than a one-off solution.
