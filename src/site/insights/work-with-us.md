> We are building the technical infrastructure for a more equitable future. If that mission resonates with you, we would love to hear from you.

Mortar is a small, ambitious team working at the intersection of data, intelligence, and public services. We design and build the digital tools that help organisations in health, housing, government, and the cultural sector understand their communities, allocate their resources, and deliver genuinely better outcomes for the people who depend on them.

We are growing, and we are looking for people who share our commitment to doing this work with rigour, creativity, and care. We do not expect you to have ticked every box. We do expect you to be curious, collaborative, and serious about the purpose behind the work.

If any of the roles below feel right for you, get in touch at [hello@mortarworks.com](mailto:hello@mortarworks.com) with a short note about yourself and why you are interested. There is no standard application process — we would rather have a conversation.

## Associate — Children and Young People

We are looking for an experienced practitioner to join us as an Associate in our Children and Young People practice. This is a consultancy-based role working alongside our product and design teams to help us develop and deliver solutions for organisations working with children and young people — from early help and family support to school inclusion and youth services.

**What you will bring:**
- Frontline or commissioning experience in children's services, education, or youth work
- A strong understanding of the policy and legislative landscape around children and young people
- The ability to translate practitioner knowledge into product and service requirements
- Experience of working in multi-agency or partnership settings
- An interest in how data and technology can support — rather than replace — the human relationships at the heart of good practice with young people

**What you will do:**
- Work with our product and design teams to shape solutions that reflect the real complexity of working with children, young people, and families
- Support client engagements, bringing practitioner credibility and expertise to discovery, design, and implementation
- Contribute to our thinking on policy, practice, and the future of services for children and young people
- Help us build and develop relationships with organisations across the sector

## Associate — Dementia Friendly Venues

We are looking for an Associate with expertise in dementia care and inclusive design to work with us on our dementia friendly venue accreditation programme and related projects. This role sits at the intersection of practice knowledge and digital design — helping us ensure that the tools and frameworks we develop genuinely serve the needs of people living with dementia, their carers, and the organisations that want to welcome them.

**What you will bring:**
- Experience in dementia care, occupational therapy, or inclusive design
- Knowledge of the principles and standards that underpin dementia friendly environments
- The ability to work with venues, cultural organisations, and public sector clients to support their dementia friendly practice
- An understanding of the challenges facing people living with dementia in navigating public spaces and services
- Experience of training, assessment, or accreditation work

**What you will do:**
- Support the development and delivery of our dementia friendly venue accreditation programme
- Work with venues and cultural organisations to assess their practice and support improvement
- Contribute to the design of the digital tools that support accreditation, training, and ongoing practice development
- Help us develop our thinking and our offer in this area as the programme grows

## Associate — Digital Skills and Employability

We are looking for an Associate with expertise in digital skills, employment support, and the barriers that prevent people from accessing economic opportunity. This role will contribute to the design and delivery of our digital skills and employability tools — helping us ensure that they reflect the real needs and circumstances of the people they are built to serve.

**What you will bring:**
- Experience working in digital inclusion, employment support, adult education, or skills provision
- An understanding of the structural barriers to digital participation and employment for people facing disadvantage
- The ability to work with commissioners, providers, and participants to shape services that are effective and accessible
- Experience of service design, facilitation, or programme development in a relevant context
- An interest in how intelligent digital tools can improve the reach and effectiveness of skills and employability services

**What you will do:**
- Work with our product and design teams on the development of digital skills and employability tools
- Support client engagements in local government, housing, and the voluntary sector
- Contribute to our evidence base on what works in digital skills and employability support
- Help us build relationships with commissioners and providers across the sector

## Product Manager — Hoop'd

We are looking for a Product Manager to lead the development of Hoop'd, our intelligent design system for connecting people and places. This is a central role in our product team — responsible for shaping the roadmap, working closely with clients and users, and ensuring that Hoop'd continues to evolve as a genuinely excellent, impactful product.

**What you will bring:**
- Product management experience, ideally in a SaaS, govtech, or social impact context
- A strong user-centred instinct — you understand that great products are built around genuine user needs, not assumed requirements
- The ability to work across design, engineering, and client teams to translate insight into a coherent, deliverable roadmap
- Experience of managing a product through its growth phase — balancing new feature development with quality, stability, and debt management
- An interest in the specific challenges of place-based digital services for public and community organisations

**What you will do:**
- Own the Hoop'd product roadmap — setting priorities, making trade-offs, and keeping the team focused on what matters most
- Work closely with clients and users to understand their needs and translate them into clear, well-specified requirements
- Collaborate with our design and engineering teams through the full product development cycle
- Track and communicate product performance — gathering feedback, measuring outcomes, and driving continuous improvement
- Contribute to the positioning and development of Hoop'd as a product in the market

## Junior Designer

We are looking for a Junior Designer to join our design team. This is an early-career role for someone who is passionate about design, curious about the intersection of digital and social impact, and ready to learn by doing on real, complex projects.

**What you will bring:**
- A strong foundation in visual or interaction design — whether from formal training or self-directed learning
- Proficiency in Figma or equivalent design tools
- An eye for detail and a commitment to producing work that is both beautiful and genuinely usable
- Curiosity and a willingness to engage seriously with complex problems in public services, health, and community
- A collaborative disposition — you work well with others, communicate your thinking clearly, and are open to feedback

**What you will do:**
- Work alongside our senior designers on the full range of Mortar's design work — from product interfaces and service design artefacts to communications and brand materials
- Contribute to user research, design critiques, and client workshops
- Develop your skills across interaction design, service design, and visual communication in a supportive, demanding environment
- Bring your own perspective and creative instincts to bear on the problems we work on
